Key Features

Revocability : The bank and client shares can be revoked and replaced at any time as long as the bank administrator is present.

Confidentiality : We use Visual Steganography to hide the shares in seemingly innnocuous images to provide our clients with the utmost confidentiality.

Ease of Use : Upon the share generation phase, the only thing the client needs to do is to upload the cheque and click sign! There is no need for the bank or client to keep on uploading their shares to reconstruct the signature.

Crystal Clear Reconstructed Images : We use state-of-the-art visual cryptography technique to encrypt, decrypt, and clean the reconstucted images. 99% of the orginal image is preserved.

0) Learning Tool

  1. Learning Tool page to learn more on Cryptography and Steganography with an included Mini Quiz at the end.


i) Pre-Setup Phase

  1. Client would need to create a vSignIt account through our website. The link to this page can be obtained when you click on 'Sign Up' in the login page.


ii) Login Page

  1. The Bank or Client would need to Login with their vSignIt account through our website to use our services.


iii) Admin Share Generation Page

The bank would register the client's signature on his behalf, which would later be split into two shares.

  1. The bank would need to 'choose the signature' it has scanned to be processed by our system.

  2. The system would check if the dimension is correct before proceeding.


iv) Client Page

  1. Client would choose their preferred Bank in the selection box menu; upload their cheque to be signed and the cheque will be sent to the Bank for approval.


v) Admin Share Reconstruction Page

The bank would see all the cheque transactions sent by the client and futher actions are required by the bank.

  1. The bank would need to either verify the cheque by clicking 'Verify' or

  2. 'Delete' if the cheque has been wrongly sent to the bank.

vi) Admin Share Verification Page

Upon, clicking 'Verify' on the individual transaction on the Admin Share Reconstruction Page, the bank would see the client's cheque that needs to be verified.

  1. The bank would need click on 'Accept' to confirm the verification or

  2. 'Reject' if the cheque has been wrongly sent to the bank.

  3. The client and bank will be notified through email on the outcome of the cheque.
